Cultural Vocabulary and Traditions Around the World

a cheongsam
Definition: A form-fitting traditional Chinese dress with a high neck and side slits, often worn for special occasions.
Example sentence: She looked elegant in her red cheongsam at the wedding celebration.
Extra fact: The bride wore a beautiful silk cheongsam embroidered with golden dragons.
Kilt
Definition: A knee-length pleated skirt, traditionally worn by men in Scotland, featuring tartan patterns.
Example sentence: At the Highland Games, the dancers wore colorful kilts and performed traditional dances.
Extra fact: A traditional Scottish kilt displays tartan patterns that are unique to different clans.
Ice Hockey
Definition: A fast-paced team sport played on ice with sticks and a puck, central to Canadian culture.
Example sentence: Canada's ice hockey team won the Olympic gold medal with exceptional teamwork.
Extra fact: Ice hockey is considered Canada's national sport and a national passion.
Kimono
Definition: A traditional Japanese garment with square sleeves, wrapped front, and an obi sash belt.
Example sentence: She wore an elegant silk kimono during the cherry blossom festival.
Extra fact: A traditional kimono is fastened with a decorative obi sash around the waist.
Origami
Definition: The Japanese art of paper folding to create intricate shapes like cranes, flowers, and animals.
Example sentence: Children learn origami in school to make paper cranes symbolizing peace and longevity.
Extra fact: Origami transforms a single sheet of paper into beautiful artistic designs.
Kimchi
Definition: A spicy fermented Korean dish made from vegetables, typically cabbage, with chili peppers and seasonings.
Example sentence: Every Korean meal includes kimchi for its tangy, spicy flavor and health benefits.
Extra fact: Kimchi is Korea's national fermented dish served at almost every meal.
Carnival (Carnevale)
Definition: A festive celebration featuring parades, masquerades, and elaborate costumes, especially famous in Venice.
Example sentence: Venice's carnival draws thousands of visitors with colorful floats and mysterious masked balls.
Extra fact: Italy's Carnival includes historic parades and festivities before the Lenten season.
Venetian Mask
Definition: An ornate, decorative disguise worn during Carnival, traditionally made with velvet, gold, and intricate designs.
Example sentence: She donned an ornate Venetian mask for the mysterious carnival ball.
Extra fact: A decorated mask allows anonymity during Venice's famous festivities.
African Drums
Definition: A percussion instrument used in African music, including varieties like djembe, talking drums, and frame drums.
Example sentence: The village celebration featured energetic rhythmic beats from traditional African drums.
Extra fact: An African drum drives communal celebrations and dances across many African cultures.
Samba
Definition: A Brazilian rhythm and dance originating in Bahia, featuring syncopated beats and hip movements, central to carnival.
Example sentence: Rio's carnival pulses with vibrant samba music and energetic dancers.
Extra fact: Samba evolved from African traditions into modern urban carnival celebrations.
Belly Dance
Definition: A Middle Eastern dance form featuring isolated movements of the hips, belly, and torso, with origins in Turkey and surrounding regions.
Example sentence: Performers showcase fluid belly dance movements with shimmies and undulations.
Extra fact: Belly dance emphasizes graceful body movements and cultural expression.
Rock Music
Definition: A guitar-driven music genre originating in the USA, blending blues, country, and rhythm & blues elements.
Example sentence: Rock music exploded globally in the 1950s with legendary artists like Elvis Presley.
Extra fact: American rock music shaped global pop culture from the mid-20th century onward.
